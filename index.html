Ladder Barrel

SYS-HW-001

Format Standard: T-013 IRON SKELETON (USPT)
Owner: The Architect (Joey)

1.0 Commander's Intent

The Friction

The Ladder Barrel is the highest-risk apparatus on the floor. There are no springs to assist or catch the client. Gravity is absolute, and improper mounting leads directly to joint sheer or falls.

The Strategy

Enforce an immutable "Three-Point Contact" rule for mounting and establish the LUMBOPELVIC alignment as the absolute prerequisite before any movement begins.

2.0 Objectives (The End State)

  • Zero Fall Risk

    Client safely mounts and dismounts using verified physical anchors.

  • Biomechanical Integrity

    Client achieves targeted mobility/extension without resorting to a posterior pelvic tilt (The "Gym Tucking" compensation).

  • Standardized Calibration

    Instructor sets the universal distance correctly on the first attempt, preventing mid-exercise adjustments.

3.0 Standard Procedures (The Execution Cycle)

Phase 1 Apparatus Calibration

1. Locate Apex
Identify the exact highest point of the barrel's curve.
2. Measure
Adjust the sliding distance so the client's sit-bones rest on the apex while their feet hook under the rung.
3. Verify the Micro-Bend
Ensure the client's knees have a slight micro-bend. If legs lock out completely, the barrel is too far. If knees bend at 90 degrees, the barrel is too close.

Phase 2 "Dual View" Mounting Protocol

1. ANCHOR
Client grips the top rung with both hands.
"Grab the top rung with both hands. This is your anchor."
2. STEP-UP
Client places one foot on the bottom wooden base.
"Step one foot onto the wooden base, keep your hands locked."
3. PIVOT
Client transfers weight to arms and pivots pelvis onto the barrel apex.
"Use your arms to lift your weight, and sit on the apex."
4. LOCK
Client slides feet between rungs and dorsiflexes to lock in.
"Hook your feet under the rung. Flex your toes up."
1. DISTANCE
Client stands one leg-length away, facing the barrel.
"Stand tall facing the barrel, about one leg-length away."
2. ANCHOR
Client places both hands flat on the barrel apex.
"Place both hands flat on the top of the barrel to brace yourself."
3. PLACEMENT
Client lifts the leg and softly places the Achilles on the curve.
"Lift your leg and place your ankle softly on the curve."

Phase 3 The Execution

Level The Pelvis (Technical)

"Instruct the client to align the ASIS and PSIS in the same horizontal plane before any movement begins."

The Shepherd Cue (Imagery)

"Your hip bones are headlights. Do not let your headlights shine up into the trees. Point them straight at the wall."

Execute: Proceed with the stretch or rotational movement only while neutral pelvis is held.
